# Realmforge Project Context

This file is the permanent game/design context for `Realmforge: Shards of the Worldheart`.

Future Codex sessions must read this file before implementing game features. The narrative version of the full story is also maintained in `docs/story-bible.md`.

## Game Premise

Realmforge is a 2D browser-based fantasy RPG web app. The player creates a highly customisable hero, explores a fractured world through a world map, completes quests, fights bosses, unlocks abilities, builds combos, gains XP and gold, and eventually decides the fate of the Worldheart.

The player fantasy is:
"I build my own fantasy character and carve my own path through a broken world."

The player does not manually write the story. Their story emerges through origin, class, affinity, abilities, quests completed, bosses defeated, regions explored, factions supported, and endings chosen.

## Full Detailed Storyline

Long ago, the realm of Elarion was held together by an ancient magical core known as the Worldheart.

The Worldheart was not just a source of magic. It was the force that balanced life, death, nature, memory, ambition, and fate. Kingdoms rose around it. Religions worshipped it. Mages studied it. Warriors fought to protect it. Every region of the world depended on it in some way.

Then, one night, the Worldheart shattered.

Nobody fully agrees on why. The holy orders claim it was broken by forbidden magic. The mages claim the kings tried to weaponise it. The druids believe the Worldheart rejected mankind's greed. The old soldiers say the gods abandoned the realm. The common people only know that since the shattering, the world has never been whole.

The land split into unstable regions. Each region was warped by a different fragment of the Worldheart. Cities became militarised fortresses. Forests began whispering. Temples sank beneath black water. Dragons returned to the coast. Monsters appeared in places where there used to be farms and roads.

The player begins as an unknown adventurer during the final years of this broken age. They are not a chosen prince, legendary knight, or person born into greatness. They are one of many people trying to survive in a fractured world.

Unlike most people, the player is Unbound. Worldheart fragments react to them. Their soul is not fixed to one destiny, one faction, one class, or one ending. They can become almost anything depending on choices, abilities, allies, and beliefs.

The main journey is simple: explore the fractured realm, defeat the corrupted rulers of each region, collect the Worldheart Shards, and decide what the world should become.

The deeper story is that power does not only reveal who the player is. Power lets the player decide what kind of world deserves to exist.

## World History

### Age of First Flame

Before kingdoms, cities, and written language, the world was wild and unstable. Magic existed everywhere, but it was chaotic. Mountains shifted, seas swallowed land, forests grew overnight and vanished by morning, and spirits, beasts, humans, and ancient beings fought for survival.

The first civilisation discovered a glowing magical core beneath the centre of the continent and called it the Worldheart. They feared it first, then learned from it. The Worldheart responded to emotion, memory, and willpower. Near it, land stabilised, crops grew, storms calmed, spirits became less hostile, and time became more predictable.

The first civilisation built Aurelmere, the Golden Capital, around it. This began the first empire.

### Age of Crowns

As the Worldheart stabilised the realm, different peoples formed kingdoms. Six great powers emerged:

- The Iron Crown: armies, cities, law, and military order.
- The Verdant Pact: forests, beasts, old nature magic, and living balance.
- The Sun Order: holy warriors who believed the Worldheart was divine.
- The Obsidian Circle: mages who studied forbidden knowledge.
- The Tidebound Clans: coastal kingdoms, sailors, raiders, and storm-callers.
- The Free Guilds: adventurers, merchants, mercenaries, and explorers.

These powers were held together by the Concord of the Heart. No faction could own the Worldheart. Each faction could draw power from it, but none could dominate it. The Worldheart belonged to the realm itself.

The realm prospered. Ironvale became the greatest military city in the east. Blackroot Forest became a sacred natural sanctuary. The Sunken Temple was a grand religious archive. Dragon Coast became a wealthy sea-trade region. Moonspire Academy became the centre of magical research. Aurelmere ruled from the centre.

Peace created ambition. The Iron Crown wanted order. The Sun Order wanted protection and worship. The Obsidian Circle wanted study and reshaping. The Verdant Pact wanted the Heart returned to nature. The Tidebound Clans wanted independence. The Free Guilds wanted access for all people.

### War of Six Banners

The War of Six Banners began after the emperor of Aurelmere died without an heir. Each faction accused the others of plotting to seize the Worldheart.

The Iron Crown marched on the capital. The Obsidian Circle opened sealed archives. The Sun Order declared a holy emergency. The Verdant Pact awakened ancient forest spirits. The Tidebound Clans blockaded the coast. The Free Guilds split into rival mercenary companies.

The war lasted nine years. By the end, cities were damaged, trade collapsed, magic became unstable, and monsters appeared more frequently.

During the final battle, all six factions reached the Worldheart chamber beneath Aurelmere. Nobody knows exactly what happened there.

Conflicting records remain:

- The Sun Order says mortal hands reached for divine power.
- The Obsidian Circle says the Worldheart was deliberately broken to prevent a greater catastrophe.
- The Verdant Pact says the realm screamed and the Heart answered.
- The Iron Crown destroyed most of its own records.

All stories agree that Ser Caldris Veyne, First Warden of the Worldheart, was the last person seen standing beside it before it broke.

After the shattering, Aurelmere vanished behind a storm of golden ash. The Worldheart split into fragments and scattered across the realm. Each fragment embedded itself in a different region, twisting that place according to the power inside it.

## Current Age: The Fractured Realm

The game begins around 100 years after the shattering.

Most people alive today never saw the old world. Roads between regions are dangerous. Factions control different territories. Magic is useful but unstable. Monsters are part of daily life. Adventurers are necessary because kingdoms can no longer protect everyone.

At the centre of the map is the sealed ruin of Aurelmere, now called the Ashen Capital. Nobody enters it and returns the same. The sky above it is permanently gold and grey. At night, strange lights rise from the ruins like memories trying to escape.

Every major faction wants access to the Ashen Capital because they believe the largest remaining piece of the Worldheart is still there. The capital cannot be opened unless someone gathers several major Worldheart Shards from across the realm.

## Player Role: The Unbound

Most people in Elarion are tied strongly to one fate. Their magic, talents, and destiny are usually shaped by bloodline, birthplace, religion, or faction.

The player is Unbound. They can adapt to different sources of power. This supports the gameplay freedom to choose origin, className, affinity, abilities, faction alignment, gear, quest path, region order, and ending ideology.

In the story, this makes the player dangerous. Some factions see them as a miracle. Some see them as a weapon. Some see them as a mistake.

Normal people touching shards may be corrupted, empowered, or destroyed. The player can absorb shard power and survive, but still suffers consequences. This allows conflicting builds: holy paladin with shadow magic, rogue becoming dragon-blood assassin, mage combining nature and blood magic, or warrior becoming more than human.

## Opening: The Ashfall Road

The game begins on the Ashfall Road, an old trade route between outer villages and Ironvale.

The player travels with a caravan of merchants, refugees, and low-rank adventurers. Origin decides why:

- Exiled Noble: forced out after being accused of treason.
- Street Thief: escaping city guards.
- Cursed Scholar: seeking records about a soul mark.
- Temple Acolyte: travelling to a ruined shrine.
- Village Hunter: searching for monsters that destroyed home.
- Mercenary: hired as cheap caravan protection.

The caravan is attacked by Hollowborn: people, beasts, and spirits warped by broken Worldheart energy. They are not mindless. Some remember fragments of who they were. Some repeat old words. Some protect places they no longer understand.

The player survives and finds a small glowing shard buried inside a broken roadside shrine. When touched, it reacts and says: "The Heart remembers you." The shard dissolves into light and enters the player.

This unlocks affinity, first ability, world map access, and first main quest: Seek the Fragments.

The player reaches Hearthmere Outpost, a fortified town built from old caravans, stone walls, and broken statues from the old empire. It becomes the first hub.

## Main Story Structure

### Act 1: The Unbound Awakens

Main goals:

- Create character.
- Survive Ashfall Road.
- Reach Hearthmere Outpost.
- Learn about Worldheart Shards.
- Choose first region.

Unlocked regions:

- Ironvale Outskirts.
- Blackroot Forest Edge.
- Old Quarry Dungeon.
- Hearthmere Ruins.

Main event:
The player absorbs the first minor shard, becomes known as Unbound, and factions begin to notice.

### Act 2: The First Shards

Main goals:

- Explore early regions.
- Complete faction quests.
- Defeat first regional bosses.
- Collect 2 major shards.
- Unlock advanced abilities.

Main regions:

- Ironvale City.
- Blackroot Forest.
- Sunken Temple Entrance.
- Dragon Coast Lowlands.

Main event:
Each shard contains power and a memory of the Worldheart's shattering. The player is collecting pieces of history.

### Act 3: The Lies of the Heart

Main goals:

- Collect 3 or more major shards.
- Unlock Moonspire Academy.
- Discover hidden records about Ser Caldris.
- Learn why the Worldheart shattered.

Main event:
The player learns Ser Caldris may have shattered the Worldheart to stop something inside it from awakening.

### Act 4: The World Changes

After enough shards are collected, the seal around the Ashen Capital weakens and triggers the Second Pulse.

Effects:

- Sky changes.
- Regions become more unstable.
- New enemies appear.
- Faction leaders become desperate.
- Some NPCs move.
- Some quests become unavailable.
- New late-game quests unlock.

World reaction examples:

- Ironvale support causes Iron Crown patrols to appear.
- Verdant support causes forests to reclaim abandoned roads.
- Obsidian support opens forbidden research sites.
- Sun Order support creates holy barriers near corrupted zones.
- Ignoring factions lets bandits and monsters spread.

### Act 5: The Ashen Capital

Main goals:

- Enter Aurelmere, the Ashen Capital.
- Face the remaining Wardens.
- Discover the truth.
- Choose final ideology.
- Defeat or replace Ser Caldris.
- Decide the ending.

Key locations:

- Hall of Crowns.
- Garden of Oaths.
- Archive of Unmade Days.
- Warden's Gate.
- Heart Chamber.

Truth:
Ser Caldris shattered the Worldheart because it was also a prison for the Hollow Star, a cosmic force of emptiness and hunger. The first civilisation built around the Worldheart without understanding it was a seal. Every use weakened the seal. The war pushed it to breaking point. Caldris scattered the seal by breaking the Heart, saving the world from immediate destruction but dooming it to slow collapse.

## The Ashen Capital Final Arc

The Ashen Capital is the final area and a broken memory of the old world.

Areas:

- Silent Gate: six faction statues react to reputation.
- Golden Streets: echoes of ordinary people before disaster.
- Hall of Crowns: faction leaders argue over the Heart.
- Archive of Unmade Days: possible futures and ending visions.
- Warden's Gate: Caldris's human memory explains the truth.
- Heart Chamber: the largest remaining piece of the Worldheart.

The Archive of Unmade Days foreshadows:

- Restored golden empire.
- Military world under one law.
- Wild realm reclaimed by nature.
- Mage-designed reality without chaos.
- Free but unstable independent regions.
- Dark world ruled by the Hollow Star.

At the Warden's Gate, Caldris explains that the Worldheart was a seal, the Hollow Star was growing stronger, the factions almost released it, and he broke the Heart to scatter the seal.

Key question:
"Now that you know why I broke the world, tell me, Unbound - would you have done better?"

At the Heart Chamber, Caldris may see the player as a worthy successor, reckless fool, tyrant in the making, corrupted vessel, or only hope left.

## Final Boss: Ser Caldris Veyne

Ser Caldris is the final test of the player's ideology. He fights because he believes nobody should have the power to decide the world's fate.

His argument:
Kings failed. Priests lied. Mages reached too far. Nature turned cruel. Free people became divided. Even he broke the world while trying to save it.

Boss phases can depend on collected shards:

- Shard of Dominion: command and defence.
- Shard of Growth: roots and spirits.
- Shard of Memory: echoes and illusions.
- Shard of Storms: lightning and speed.
- Shard of Will: reality shifts.
- Shard of Oaths: spectral guardians.

Ending prompt:
"Then choose. Not as hero. Not as ruler. As the one who must live with what follows."

## Ending Paths

### Restore the Worldheart

The player rebuilds the Worldheart. The realm stabilises, corruption fades, roads become safer, and magic balances. Centralised power also returns.

Best for:
Sun Order path, balanced hero path, players who value healing and unity.

Consequence:
The Hollow Star remains sealed, but old mistakes may repeat.

Final message:
"The Heart beats again. Whether it beats for all, or for those who claim to speak for all, remains the question of the next age."

### Claim the Worldheart

The player absorbs the completed Worldheart and becomes the central source of magic.

Best for:
Obsidian Circle path, Iron Crown path, power-focused builds, players who believe one strong ruler can save the world.

Consequence:
The realm survives, but freedom weakens.

Final message:
"The world no longer trembles. It waits. Every dawn now begins with your permission."

### Break the Worldheart Forever

The player destroys the remaining Worldheart power. Magic fades, the Hollow Star loses its path, factions lose their strongest power, and people rebuild with ordinary strength.

Best for:
Free Guild path, anti-faction path, players who distrust ancient power.

Consequence:
Many magical beings fade, some regions suffer, but the world is free from the Worldheart cycle.

Final message:
"For the first time in an age, the realm is silent. Not dead. Waiting to learn how to live without gods beneath its feet."

### Return the Heart to the Land

The player spreads the Heart through nature, rivers, mountains, forests, and living beings. No faction can control it.

Best for:
Verdant Pact path, nature affinity builds, players who support Liora.

Consequence:
Civilisation survives but cannot dominate. The realm becomes more mystical and less predictable.

Final message:
"The Heart no longer sits on a throne beneath stone. It beats in root, rain, claw, breath, and bone."

### The Iron Dominion

The player uses the Worldheart to unite the realm under one command structure. Roads reopen, trade returns, armies protect villages, and rebellion is crushed.

Best for:
Iron Crown loyalists, military builds, players who value order over freedom.

Consequence:
The world is safe but watched.

Final message:
"The realm kneels, and in kneeling, it survives. Whether survival is enough is left to those born beneath your banners."

### The Hollow Ascension

The player accepts the Hollow Star's power and opens the Heart fully.

Best for:
Forbidden magic path, blood/shadow/warlock builds, players choosing power at any cost.

Consequence:
The world becomes alien and frightening. The player wins, but it is unclear whether they remain themselves.

Final message:
"The stars above Elarion vanish one by one. In their place, a new light opens its eye."

### The Fractured Peace

The player refuses all absolute solutions and divides shards among trusted factions, regions, and guardians.

Best for:
Balanced faction reputation, diplomatic choices, players completing many NPC questlines.

Consequence:
The world remains imperfect but alive. No single power dominates.

Final message:
"The realm remains fractured, but no longer falling. Perhaps wholeness was never the same as peace."

## Origin-Based Story Hooks

- Exiled Noble: Ironvale nobles recognise, insult, or try to use the player. Quests include recovering house name, exposing betrayal, becoming a political leader, or rejecting nobility.
- Street Thief: smugglers, refugees, and lower-city NPCs trust the player more. Quests include joining/destroying a thieves' network, helping refugees cross borders, or stealing a relic.
- Cursed Scholar: Obsidian Circle NPCs are interested and Sun Order NPCs are cautious. Quests include researching the curse, learning forbidden spells, and discovering the curse links to a shard.
- Temple Acolyte: Sun Order NPCs trust the player early but may reject forbidden powers. Quests include recovering relics, protecting pilgrims, and deciding whether faith matters after truth.
- Village Hunter: common people respect the player and nobles underestimate them. Quests include hunting monsters, protecting settlements, and tracking the creature that destroyed home.
- Mercenary: guilds and soldiers treat the player professionally. Quests include choosing contract or conscience, building an adventurer company, and becoming a faction champion.

## Gameplay Loop

CA1 core gameplay loop:

1. User creates profile.
2. User creates character.
3. User views world regions.
4. User views quests in a region.
5. User attempts quest.
6. Server calculates result using character stats, quest difficulty, level, and future ability/faction bonuses.
7. Character gains XP and gold.
8. Character may level up.
9. Adventure log is saved.
10. User can view progression.

CA1 core mechanic:
Character creation plus map-based quest progression.

CA2 second core mechanic:
Combo battle system plus ability unlock/progression.

## Core Mechanics

### Character Creation and World Map Quest Progression

This is the CA1 core mechanic. It must be implemented first through backend APIs and later surfaced through the CA2 frontend.

Server responsibilities:

- Validate users and character ownership.
- Calculate stats from origin, className, and affinity.
- Read regions and quests from database.
- Resolve quest attempts using explainable rules.
- Persist XP, gold, level, and adventure logs.

### Combo Battle System and Ability Progression

This is the CA2 second mechanic. Do not implement it before the correct ticket.

Design:

- Abilities have roles: opener, chain, finisher, defensive, utility, ultimate.
- Combo tags allow meaningful ability sequences.
- Unlocks are tied to level, className, affinity, and story progression.
- Frontend should animate combo results with Web Animations API.

Example combos:

- Spellblade Fire: Ash Step -> Flame Slash -> Phoenix Burst.
- Rogue Shadow: Vanish -> Backstab -> Eclipse Cut.
- Paladin Holy: Shield Oath -> Smite -> Divine Judgement.
- Necromancer Oath: Gravecall -> Spirit Bind -> Final Vow.

## Ability Progression Themes

Fire:
Ambition, destruction, renewal, rebellion. Early abilities include Spark, Flame Slash, Burning Bolt. Mid abilities include Wildfire Chain, Phoenix Guard, Ash Step. Late abilities include Worldflame Nova and Crown of Embers.

Shadow:
Secrecy, survival, forbidden truth, identity. Early abilities include Vanish, Shadow Cut, Blind Spot. Mid abilities include Nightbind, Umbral Clone, Silence Mark. Late abilities include Eclipse Form and Hollow Step.

Holy:
Faith, order, sacrifice, restoration. Early abilities include Bless, Smite, Lightward. Mid abilities include Radiant Chain, Judgement Seal, Prayer of Resolve. Late abilities include Sunheart Ascension and Divine Reckoning.

Storm:

## Backend Design Intent

Use an interview-friendly Express architecture:

- `src/server.js`: starts the server.
- `src/app.js`: configures Express, JSON parsing, routes, and middleware.
- `src/db/client.js`: creates the libSQL/Drizzle connection.
- `src/db/schema.js`: defines database tables.
- `src/db/seed.js`: seeds story-rich data.
- `src/models`: database access functions only.
- `src/controllers`: request validation, control flow, status codes.
- `src/routes`: Express route definitions only.
- `src/utils`: reusable helpers such as ids, validation, leveling, and game rules.
- `src/middlewares`: not found, error handler, and later auth middleware.

Rules:

- Use Drizzle ORM for database operations.
- Keep SQL/database logic out of controllers where reasonable.
- Return consistent JSON errors.
- Never return passwords.
- Use correct HTTP status codes.
- Every endpoint must be manually testable.
- Keep code readable enough to explain in an interview.

## Leveling Rules

Use a simple explainable formula:

- Every 100 XP equals 1 level.
- On level up, increase HP by 10.
- Optional future rule: increase one or more stats by 1.

Keep this easy to explain in the CA interview.

## Adventure Attempt Logic

`POST /adventures/attempt` body:

```json
{
  "userId": "...",
  "characterId": "...",
  "questId": "..."
}
```

Logic:

1. Validate user exists.
2. Validate character exists.
3. Validate character belongs to user.
4. Validate quest exists.
5. Check character level is at least quest required level.
6. Read required stat from `quest.required_stat`.
7. Get the matching character stat.
8. Success if character stat plus level bonus is at least `quest.required_stat_value`.
9. On success, grant full XP and gold.
10. On failure, grant small XP and reduced or no gold.
11. Update character XP, gold, and level.
12. Optionally update user XP, gold, and level.
13. Insert adventure log.
14. Return outcome, rewards, level changes, and story result text.

## CA1 Implementation Strategy

1. Build real Express and database foundation.
2. Implement users CRUD first because it satisfies several CA1 requirements.
3. Implement characters as the FK-backed game table.
4. Implement regions and quests as world map content.
5. Implement adventure attempt mechanic as the CA1 core gameplay loop.
6. Implement abilities after the quest loop so CA2 has a clean progression base.
7. Validate CA1 requirements with endpoint tests and update docs.

Do not implement CA2 auth or frontend during CA1 tickets unless the user explicitly asks.

## CA2 Implementation Strategy

1. Add bcrypt and jsonwebtoken.
2. Implement auth endpoints.
3. Hash all new passwords.
4. Never return passwords.
5. Add reusable auth middleware.
6. Protect routes that require login.
7. Add plain HTML/CSS/JS frontend using Fetch API.
8. Surface core mechanic 1 through frontend.
9. Implement and surface core mechanic 2: combo battle system plus ability unlock/progression.
10. Update ERD and docs.

## Main Villain Philosophy

There is no simple evil villain until the Hollow Star.

Each major leader has a believable reason:

- Lord Marshal Voss believes order saves lives.
- Elder Vaun believes civilisation failed.
- Prior Eamon believes truth can destroy society if revealed carelessly.
- Archmagister Selene believes free will creates repeated disaster.
- Ser Caldris believes nobody should be trusted with ultimate power.

The Hollow Star represents the temptation to erase struggle by becoming something beyond human.

This makes bosses ideological, not just monsters.
